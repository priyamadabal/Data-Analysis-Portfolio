Spend Analysis & Dashboarding

Project Overview

This project involved analyzing company spending data using Python and Power BI to uncover cost trends, identify key suppliers, and suggest actionable savings opportunities. The analysis covered data cleansing, KPI extraction, and interactive dashboard design using Power BI.

Objectives

  • Clean and preprocess raw spend data for analysis
  • Generate supplier-based insights using Python
  • Build interactive Power BI dashboards for spend categories, supplier distribution, and savings suggestions
  • Questions to help structure a spend analysis report for clients

Tools & Technologies

Python (pandas, seaborn) Power BI Excel Data Cleaning Visualization

Jupyter Notebook Analysis

The notebook (Python .ipynb) presents a comprehensive analysis, starting from raw data exploration to identifying cost-saving opportunities. Key findings include:

  • Data profiling, null value handling, and duplicate detection.
  • Spend breakdown by supplier, category, and sub-category.
  • Calculated top 5 vendors: US bank, PERA, CIGNA HEALTH AND LIFE INSURANCE COMPANY, FIDELITY NATIONAL TITLE COMPANY and VANTAGEPOINT and their total spend in descending order.
  • Calculated top 5 vendors for 2023 and total spend with each in descending order of spend.
  • Found that IRS, MARK YOUNG CONSTRUCTION, LLC and ARCHER WESTERN CONSTRUCTION LLC vendors have had the largest increase in overall spend from 2022 to 2023. Their value of the increase for each vendor is displayed.
  • Top 3 accounts Capital: Bldgs & Bldg Equip, Capital: Water Right Acq and R&M: Bldgs & Bldg Equip have had the largest increase in overall spend from 2022 to 2023.
  • A month-on-month trend plot for total spend for Repairs & Maintenance and their observations have been highlighted.
  • Pareto analysis showing top 20% suppliers contribute to 80% of total spend.
  • Grouped summaries and pivot-style aggregations for deeper insight. Supplier consolidation opportunities based on tail spend analysis.

Power BI Analysis

The Power BI dashboard (Download .pbix File) provides a visual and interactive layer over the spend data, helping stakeholders quickly identify high-spend vendors, regional patterns, and opportunities for optimization. It complements the Python notebook by offering a user-friendly interface to slice and drill down into data.

  • KPI cards highlight total spend, average spend per vendor, and highest spend category.
  • Bar charts and tree maps display spend distribution across vendors, categories, and years.
  • Interactive filters allow users to analyze spend across different regions and time periods.
  • Spend trends by year and month provide insight into procurement seasonality.

Client-Facing Questions and Considerations

  • Understand client expectations from the spend analysis
  • Identify departments prioritized for cost-cutting
  • Investigate why November has the highest spend each year
  • Clarify total assigned budget per department (monthly/yearly)
  • Check platforms used (Azure, AWS, Oracle) and explore cost-efficient alternatives
  • Review vendor charges and suggest lower-cost alternatives if needed
  • Detect and handle data inconsistencies such as nulls, duplicates, and type mismatches
  • Impute missing values based on data size and relevance
  • Maintain weekly client updates and progress tracking via SharePoint
  • Provide a live Power BI dashboard for ongoing analysis visibility
  • Identify and filter sensitive information like salary or wage references using keywords
